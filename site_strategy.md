# Digital Therapy Website Strategy

## Strategic Positioning

Digital Therapy should be positioned as a **private operating-layer and transformation partner for modern family offices**. The site should communicate that Digital Therapy does not simply sell software; it designs and implements a unified, secure, automated operating environment across data, workflows, reporting, accounting, documents, governance, and AI enablement.

The website should be competitive with Masttro at the category level while remaining original in copy, structure, and design. Masttro’s site emphasizes family-office software, comprehensive wealth visibility, platform modules, data feeds, and security. Digital Therapy should differentiate by emphasizing the **Fusion Team**, consultative operating design, AI-enabled automation, partner-friendly delivery, and private deployment options.

## Primary Audiences

| Audience | Core Question | Website Response |
|---|---|---|
| Single family offices | How do we reduce operating friction without losing control? | Digital Therapy builds a secure private operating layer across assets, entities, data, workflows, and reports. |
| Multi-family offices | How can we serve complex clients without adding manual burden? | Digital Therapy helps standardize diagnostics, reporting, automation, and workflow orchestration. |
| Private banks and wealth advisors | How can we solve client complexity without disrupting the advisory relationship? | Digital Therapy works as a confidential implementation partner behind or beside the trusted advisor. |
| Family-office executives | How do we get faster answers with leaner teams? | Digital Therapy converts fragmented systems and manual workflows into current, governed intelligence. |

## Recommended One-Page Site Structure

| Section | Purpose | Visual Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Header | Establish premium brand and clear navigation | Light background, Digital Therapy logo, concise nav, blue private briefing CTA. |

| Security and Control | Address family-office trust concerns | Light section with private deployment, role-based access, auditability, secure integration. |

## Copy Principles

The site should use concise executive language. It should avoid long text blocks, invented metrics, direct Masttro phrasing, or generic SaaS claims. Each section should have a single primary sentence, a short supporting sentence, and visually scannable proof points. CTAs should say **Request a private briefing**, **Explore partner model**, and **Discuss a client opportunity**.

## Visual System Commitment

The selected design philosophy is quiet luxury interface design influenced by private banking, contemporary architecture, and high-end annual reports. The site should use warm ivory, white, light gray, charcoal, black, and Digital Therapy blue. Typography should combine refined display moments with precise sans-serif interface text. Every implementation file should include a top comment reminding future edits to preserve this philosophy.
