# Digital Therapy Website Research Notes

## Masttro Public Website Observations

Masttro’s homepage positions the company as family-office software for comprehensive wealth visibility across assets, entities, and generations. Its information architecture emphasizes audience segmentation, platform capability depth, trust signals, security, and a direct consultation call to action. The observed top navigation includes Who We Serve, Platform, Resources, Company, Why Masttro, Pricing, and Speak with us.

The site’s content rhythm moves from a broad platform promise to audience-specific use cases, then to quantified trust proof, family-office origin story, capability inventory, security foundation, platform exploration, insights, FAQ, and conversion CTA. This structure is useful as a competitive reference, but the Digital Therapy site should not imitate the exact copy, feature ordering, or visual styling. Digital Therapy should instead position itself as a **consultative operating-layer and end-to-end transformation partner** for family offices, not merely a software platform.

| Reference Pattern | What It Signals | Digital Therapy Adaptation |
|---|---|---|
| Hero promise centered on family-office complexity | Immediate category relevance | Lead with “private operating layer for modern family offices” and connect systems, workflows, reporting, and AI transformation. |
| Audience tabs for family offices, MFOs, advisors, institutions | Partner-channel awareness | Create sections for Family Offices, Wealth Advisors, Private Banks, and Multi-Family Offices. |
| Statistics and trust proof | Credibility and scale | Use more qualitative proof if specific Digital Therapy metrics are not available; avoid fabricating numbers. |
| Platform modules | Clear buyer understanding | Present Digital Therapy capability pillars: Operating Layer, Automation, Reporting, AI Enablement, Security, Implementation. |
| Security section | Critical family-office concern | Elevate privacy, control, on-premises/private-cloud options, governance, and permissioning. |
| Consultation CTA | Enterprise sales conversion | Use “Request a private briefing” and “Explore partner model” as premium CTAs. |

## Digital Therapy Differentiation Direction

Digital Therapy should emphasize that large family offices rarely fail because of a lack of software. They fail because data, advisors, documents, reporting cycles, governance workflows, and stakeholder expectations are fragmented. The website should therefore frame Digital Therapy as the team that designs and implements the practical operating layer above existing systems. This creates distance from direct software-only comparisons and supports partnerships with private banks, Bernstein-style advisors, Bessemer-style fiduciary organizations, and other wealth advisory channels.

The design should remain light and premium, using the user’s specified palette: black and charcoal text, white and off-white surfaces, very light gray separation, and blue as the primary accent. The site should be visual, with large boardroom/family-office imagery, abstract blue data architecture visuals, and concise executive copy.
